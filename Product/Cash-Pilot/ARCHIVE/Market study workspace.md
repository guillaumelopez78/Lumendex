## UX Principles

To ensure users rely on this data for high-stakes decisions, the UI must follow these pillars:

<aside>

### **Comprehension**

This should be intuitively understandable by the none accounting-trained SMB.

Less frontloaded “abstract” numbers. And more visually digestible info.

</aside>

<aside>

### **Trust**

Every data point in the graph must be clickable.

If the app predicts a $10k balance, the user must be able to see exactly which invoices and historical trends created that number.

</aside>

<aside>

### **Transparency**

Forecasts are estimates, not guarantees. We are transparent about the degree of certainty in our data. By labeling data based on its source:

From “Guaranteed” (known data) to “Estimated” (algorithmic), we provide the context necessary for informed decision-making. 

</aside>

<aside>

### **Supportive communication**

To avoid dragging the user through anxiety each and every time something less than optimal happens or we show or notify of financial aspects.

We’ll lead with supportive + constructive communication, that should both empower and calm the user.

</aside>

<aside>

### **No setup**

Once data are connected to our solution (invoicing and/or banking data), users should have to set up the solution any further for it to work.

Only advanced features meant for “Tax and accounting experts” could need specific setup made by the user.

</aside>---
