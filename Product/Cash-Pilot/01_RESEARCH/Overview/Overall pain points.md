# Overall pain points

### **1. Fragmented Data Across Multiple Currencies and Platforms**

- **The Core Problem:** Users managing international clients struggle with data fragmented across multiple bank accounts and currencies that do not sync with their main accounting dashboard.
- **The 'Emotional/Business Cost':** Significant manual effort is required to track balances across platforms; the user must manually transfer funds and "keep the operations in their head," leading to a lack of centralized visibility.
- **Evidence:** "So only the dedicated bank accounts for currencies that are not available in Kontist which is everything... but euro... then in my brain and all the operations are happening from Kontist for me".

---

### **2. Anxiety and Conservative Spending Due to Tax Uncertainty**

- **The Core Problem:** Users maintain excessively high cash buffers and delay investments because they cannot accurately predict upcoming quarterly prepayments, VAT, and income tax.
- **The 'Emotional/Business Cost':** High levels of "financial anxiety" and a "scared rabbit" mindset; users miss out on investment opportunities or personal salary because they fear an unexpected tax bill will cause an overdraft.
- **Evidence:** "I tend to be more conservative of what I pay myself and also money that I would do something else with invest it... because I'm always nervous about not having enough money in the contest account to pay my taxes".

---

### **3. Inability to Forecast Long-term Patterns and Seasonality**

- **The Core Problem:** Business owners struggle to predict "dry spells" or "peak seasons," relying on mental models or past spreadsheets that are difficult to correlate with current bank balances. Big question is their current "burn rate" sustainable through the low season.
- **The 'Emotional/Business Cost':** Users experience stress during quiet months (January/February) and lack the confidence to take vacations or make large purchases like equipment or vehicles.
- **Evidence:** "I have a burn rate of 300,000 DKK a month. So if I'm not getting 300,000 DKK a month, then it's going very fast... just as soon as you get into our low season".

---

### **4. Manual Spreadsheet Fatigue and Syncing Lag**

- **The Core Problem:** Users must manually update Excel or AirTable spreadsheets because their banking app does not provide a future-looking visualization of upcoming invoices.
- **The 'Emotional/Business Cost':** Lost time due to "manual work" and "admin burden"; users feel the process is "dreadful" and mentally draining, but feel forced to do it to maintain control.
- **Evidence:** "I have a spreadsheet... I really don't want to... I just wish to outsource it from the back of my head to the front of my eyes".

---

### **5. Lack of "What-If" Capability for Major Financial Decisions**

- **The Core Problem:** Users cannot simulate the impact of major expenses (hiring, buying a car, or investing in a pension) on their future liquidity.
- **The 'Emotional/Business Cost':** Decision paralysis; users feel "financially insecure" and lack the "learning component" required to grow their business rationally.
- **Evidence:** "I don't feel financially secured enough to actually put money into my pension account which I would love to but I don't have enough information. I have data but there is a mess around it".

---

### **6. Poor Recognition of Recurring Costs/Patterns**

- **The Core Problem:** Automated systems often fail to correctly categorize recurring expenses (subscriptions, rent, annual insurance), leading to "noisy" and inaccurate forecasts. Or a user forgets that a large €100,000 insurance bill is due every January 1st because the software doesn't flag it as a recurring trend from previous years.
- **The 'Emotional/Business Cost':** Being "surprised" by large outgoings, leading to unexpected overdrafts or the need to borrow money urgently.
- **Evidence:** "I expect the dashboard to clean the noise of smaller one-time spendings... it doesn't recognize the same supplier in the past... it just jumps to general costs".

# Persona types

### **The "Anxious Micro-Manager": Fear of the Unknown**

These users are primarily driven by a need for "peace of mind" and would like to use cash flow forecasting to mitigate deep-seated financial anxiety.

- **Anna Kuliberda:** She views herself as a "control freak" and an "anxious person" who needs to "outsource" financial operations from the back of her head to a visual dashboard. Her primary struggle is **data fragmentation** caused by international currencies (Wise vs. Euro accounts), which forces her to hold all operations in her head.
- **Sabine Devins:** Her pain point is **conservative stagnation**. Because she is nervous about upcoming quarterly prepayments and business taxes, she keeps a massive cash buffer in her account rather than paying herself more or investing. She specifically needs a 3–12 month view to feel "comfortable" making a personal purchase.
- **Bastiaan Doedee:** His struggle is **personal-professional intersectionality**. As a freelancer with spousal and child support obligations, he needs a forecast not just for business health, but to ensure he doesn't exceed income thresholds that would trigger legal recalculations of his maintenance payments. He currently experiences "4 hours of crying" during manual tax filing due to the complexity of these rules.

### **The "Seasonality Navigator": Managing the Burn Rate**

For these business owners, the pain is rooted in the "peaks and valleys" of their industry, where a lack of forecasting leads to survival concerns during "quiet" months.

- **Anders Rutkjær:** He manages a carpentry business with multiple employees and feels the stress of the "low season" in winter. His primary pain is **tracking obligations** like "Moms" (VAT), leasing payments, insurance costs and holiday pay for employees, which can lead to bankruptcy if not anticipated.
- **Jay Holm:** He faces **credit-driven uncertainty**. Because his car sound products are seasonal (March–October), he often has large amounts of cash "out there" with US and European customers who pay sporadically or in stages. He needs pattern recognition to know when those specific dollars will actually land in his bank.
- **Pilu Kasper Bech:** As an accountant/founder, his pain point is **customer volatility**. He sees his own clients struggle when they buy new equipment (like cars) without understanding how it will affect their liquidity in 14 days.

### **The "Skeptical Expert": Precision over Automation**

These individuals often have a financial background and struggle with current software because it lacks transparency or allows for "noisy" data.

- **Rasmus Larsen:** He is a vocal critic of "artificial stupidity." His pain is a **lack of trust in black-box automation**. He refuses to use a forecasting tool unless he can click every data point on a graph to see exactly where the numbers came from.
- **Herke Oudeboon:** A financial risk manager who finds existing software **unreliable for irregular income**. He ignores forecasts that don't allow him to manually adjust for the fact that his freelance projects start and stop at specific intervals. He specifically wants a "cash burn" signal to know exactly when he would go broke if he did nothing.
- **Harry Schutte:** With 40 years of experience, he views the current lack of integrated forecasting as a **communication gap**. He sees his clients panic because they can't connect their daily bookkeeping to a long-term profit and loss statement.
