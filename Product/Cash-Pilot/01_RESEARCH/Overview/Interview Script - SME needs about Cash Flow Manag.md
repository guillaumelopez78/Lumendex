# Interview Script - SME needs about Cash Flow Management Tool

# Objectives of the interview

**Find out what are the Small Businesses’ needs and expectations regarding a cash flow management tools:**

1. How do they manage their cash flow today?
2. What are they looking for:
    1. What information?
    2. What feature?t
3. How would they use a cash flow management tool:
    1. How often?
    2. To what end?

**Target duration for the interview: 60 minutes**

# Script

## **Introduction**

- Request permission to record (audio) and explain confidentiality.
- State the objectives of the interview:
    - Understand how a cash flow forecast could help them in the management of their company
    - How and often they would use this kind of tool/information
    - What information are key for them
- Remind them of the duration of the interview (60 minutes) and that they can stop at any time.

## **Detailed Script**

1. **Company and people personas (5 minutes)**
    - Can you briefly introduce your company? (sector, size, number of employees, year founded)
    - What is your exact role within the company?
    - What is your company's approximate annual turnover? (or range) Probes:
    - Do you use an accountant? In-house/external?
    - What is the split of responsibilities between your accountant and you?
2. **Current Practices and Challenges (15 minutes)**
    - Can you describe how you currently monitor your cash flow? (all tools used: Excel, accounting software, online banking, notebook…)
    - How often do you check your cash flow? (daily, weekly, monthly, sporadically)
    - What information do you check when it comes to cash flow? (if possible, specify if it is past, present or forecast data)
    - What takes up most of your time in cash flow supervision?
    - Once you have checked your cash flow, what concrete action do you take to adjust company financial state? (answers might be: send reminder to customers, delay payments, borrow, reduce expenses...)?
3. **Needs and Desired Information (15 minutes)**
    - Which KPIs do you want to track in priority? (available cash, runway in days, upcoming receipts, invoices to pay, customer/CA ratio…)
    - What date range is interesting for you to observe?
    - What alerts would you like to receive? (cash flow thresholds, upcoming invoices, predictable overdraft…)
    - Would you like to be able to test "what-if" scenarios? For example: "if I delay this payment by 15 days" or "if I lose 20% of revenue"
    - Would you like the tool to provide actionable suggestions (depending on the cash flow situation). Examples:
        - Loans
        - Investments,
        - Recovery tool,
        - Obtaining state subsidies assistant
    - What would be your expectations about the cash flow forecast tool, regarding: accuracy of the forecast? KPIs accuracy?
    - How would a perfect cash flow forecasting tool look according to you?
4. **Adoption, Operational Use, and Prioritization (15 minutes)**
    - How often would you open this tool if it met your needs? (daily, weekly, monthly)
    - In what situations would you use it? (preparing meetings, loan applications, daily management…)
    - On what device (web/mobile) do you think a cash flow forecast would be interesting to you?
    - What are the potential barriers to using a new tool? (cost, complexity, integration time, data security, forecast reliability)
    - If I ask you to prioritize 6 potential features, how would you rank them (quick exercise):
        - Cash flow forecast curve
        - Invoice-by-invoice view (receipts and payments)
        - Scenarios / what-if
        - Alerts / notifications
        - Action recommendations (e.g., financing request)
        - Automatic bank / accounting integration Suggested method: ask the participant to assign 1 (less important) to 6 (more important) or allocate 100 points among the options.
    - Would you be willing to pay for this tool? If so, what monthly price range would seem acceptable for an SME like yours?

## **Conclusion and Thanks**

- Is there anything else you'd like to add about cash flow management or a tool that would help you?
- May I contact you again if I have further questions?
- Thank the interviewee for its time and insightful answers.
