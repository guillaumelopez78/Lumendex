# Bastiaan Doedee

Business: Freelance Interpreter
Conclusion: Bastiaan wants a product that offer proactive, legally informed cash flow forecasting and tax assistance for Dutch freelancers.

The core opportunity is to address the following:

• Mitigate High-Stakes Financial Risk: Bastiaan's most significant need is a tool that helps him navigate complex income thresholds related to spousal maintenance and child support, which currently forces him to manage his work and vacation schedule manually.

• Simplify Tax Compliance: By replicating the success of the current sales tax app with the more complex annual income tax assessment—acting as a "translator" for Dutch government terminology and providing clear overviews—the product can solve a major source of anxiety and time-wasting.

• Provide Actionable Guidance: The desire for accurate, high-quality advice—such as "should I move this invoice to the next year?" or "is this cost worth the tax deduction?"—is high enough that Bastiaan is willing to pay a premium for it.

In summary, the ideal solution for Bastiaan is not just a reporting tool, but a proactive financial co-pilot that addresses the emotional and legal stress points of running a freelance business in the Netherlands. He values accuracy, mobile usability, and a single, centralized platform for all his administrative, tax, and future planning needs.

Pain points: 1. High-Stakes Financial/Legal Management

• Spousal Maintenance/Child Support Thresholds: The most critical pain point is the need to actively manage his annual income to avoid triggering a recalculation of spousal maintenance and child support. He is forced to "keep a watchful eye" on his income increase and sometimes plans non-working vacations at the end of the year specifically to keep his revenue below a certain threshold.

• Income Fluctuation Stress: His income is heavily influenced by the seasonal housing market and divorce rates, leading to a "panic button" moment in January (low income) and high stress due to significant volatility between months.

2. Complex and Time-Consuming Tax Assessment

• Difficulty with Income Tax Projection: The Dutch income tax system is extremely complicated, with numerous variables (mortgage interest deduction, child deduction, changes in freelance rules), making an accurate personal projection "nearly impossible" through simple apps.

• Manual and Stressful Tax Filing: He does his own annual tax assessment, which involves "4 hours of crying" due to the complexity and lack of clarity on terminology (e.g., matching the beginning and end balances for a tax year).

3. Administrative and Usability Gaps

• Mobile App Limitation for Invoices: He finds it "annoying" that he cannot upload client-generated service overviews (invoices he is to be paid) on the mobile T app and must switch to his MacBook to complete this step, which is a break in his desired workflow.

• Lack of Centralized Financial/Tax Knowledge: Both he and his colleagues struggle to find reliable, simple information on tax rules (like car deductions or pension savings) and often resort to unreliable sources like Reddit, rather than the official government website.

4. Need for Proactive Financial Guidance

• Lack of Actionable Advice: He is seeking a tool that can combine his banking data and business statistics with real advice to optimize his finances, such as tips on when to defer an invoice to the next year for tax/legal benefits, or general guidance on the value of tax-deductible expenses.

• Desire for Forecasting: As a "control freak" who checks his cash flow daily, he is anxious for a reliable, motivating forecast that predicts his income for the quarter, semester, or even the full year.

The interview transcript summarizes a discussion about cash flow management and potential features for a new financial forecasting app, centered on the experience of Bastiaan Doedee, a freelance interpreter in the Netherlands.Bastiaan's Business and Role
• Business: Bastiaan is a freelance interpreter, primarily for civil law notaries, and operates a language agency (Dalbur Mr.) with a translator colleague, Natalie.

- Role Split: Bastiaan handles interpreting, terminology, and on-site client issues, while Natalie acts as the office manager, manages paper translations, and oversees the financials (invoicing, which Bastiaan checks). They remain legally separate freelancers.Current Financial Management
- Cash Flow: He checks his real-time bank account daily. He uses the T app for all invoices and expenses but must use a web browser to upload client-generated service overviews (what he will be paid in about a month) to complete his cash flow picture.
- Sales Tax (VAT): He uses the T app's accurate overview to track the 21% sales tax, sets aside the money monthly, and pays the tax authorities quarterly. He considers this system "gold."
• Income Tax: Due to the complexity of Dutch tax rules and the app's limitations, he projects his annual income tax manually based on a revenue goal, previous year's taxes, and tracking current political changes that affect freelancers. He sets the projected amount aside in a separate account.Desired Features for Cash Flow Forecast ToolBastiaan's most critical need for a forecast tool is driven by legal obligations and income fluctuation:
- Income Thresholds (Crucial): Forecasts and tips are needed to manage his income to avoid exceeding thresholds that could trigger recalculations of spousal maintenance or child support. This allows him to strategically plan time off (e.g., taking vacation at year-end) to regulate his annual income.
- Fluctuation Analysis: Monthly, quarterly, or semester views would be useful to track income volatility related to the housing market (civil law notary work) and divorces. He notes a panic-inducing low in January and a high between May and September.
- Actionable Tips/Scenarios: He is interested in "AI-generated" tips or scenarios, such as moving an invoice to the next year, to optimize his income for tax or legal purposes.
- Tax Assessment Assistance: He would be willing to pay for an app feature that "translates" the complex terminology and questions of the annual Dutch income tax assessment, similar to how the T app handles the sales tax overview.
- Other Value-Adds: Bundling information about investing (e.g., pension) and offering an FAQ/knowledge base for simple tax rules (e.g., car deductions) would be beneficial, as many colleagues lack this information.Usage and Barriers
- Check Frequency: He would check the forecast monthly to monitor income, expenses, and divorce-related thresholds. He would also check it quarterly for sales tax.
- Device: His preferred device for convenience is a mobile app on a touch screen (iPad OS).
- Barriers: The main barrier is not the price but the accuracy and quality of the legal/financial advice provided. He suggests a good forecast and legal advice could be complimentary, perhaps bundled into a higher-tier membership fee.---
