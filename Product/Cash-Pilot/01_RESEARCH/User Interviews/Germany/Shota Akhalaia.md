Pain points: 
• Financial Compliance and Fines: The high-stakes challenge of correctly generating reports and invoices for government platforms to avoid errors and potential "big fines."

• German Tax Complexity: Difficulty understanding and complying with German tax policies; he needs automatic calculation and clear information on future tax payments (VAT, etc.).

• Need for Reliable Data: He will only adopt the new feature if the forecast is highly reliable with real, precise estimations, not just general statistics.

• Cost vs. Value: He is sensitive to the cost of the subscription, especially when features are not actively used, and values time-saving automation over manual, non-essential features like simulations.

Tool Purpose and Key Features

- Goal: The feature uses banking and invoice data to create a forecast of professional finance and liquidity, focusing on future estimations.
- Essential Features:
◦ Reliable Forecast: Shota stressed the need for a forecast based on real invoices and precise estimation, not just summarized or statistical data.
◦ Automatic Tax/VAT Information: He would find it very useful for the tool to automatically calculate and provide clear information on future tax/VAT payments, especially due to the complexity of German tax policies.
◦ Alerts: He would find notifications or alerts useful if his balance is predicted to drop below zero.
◦ Simulation: The ability to run "fictional" scenarios (simulations) was deemed an interesting extra feature for insights, but not a mandatory requirement due to his lack of time.

User's Business and Financial Context

- Occupation: Shota is a freelance developer with a small startup based in Estonia, currently in the process of developing a product. He also works on international projects via a platform like EPAM (similar to Upwork).
- Bookkeeping: His spouse, who is a professional, handles the company's bookkeeping and financial aspects using a service/tool called NENY.O. The startup currently uses a Wise business account, but Shota plans to use the Contist account in the future if he secures German clients or projects.
- Usage Frequency: If he actively starts using the feature, he anticipates checking it a minimum of one time per month, especially when monthly reports are due.
- Platform Preference: He is open to using both mobile and desktop, but prefers the desktop environment for a wider view of the information.

Willingness to Pay and Main Pain Point

- High Value Feature: Shota indicated he would definitely be willing to pay for a tool that helps to correctly generate financial reports and invoices, noting that past issues with Estonian reports required corrections, and doing this correctly is critical to avoid fines.
- Forecast Value: He suggested a one-month forecast range would be interesting, similar to cost management features he uses in cloud platforms like Azure.---
