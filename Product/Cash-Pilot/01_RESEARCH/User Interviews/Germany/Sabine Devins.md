# Sabine Devins

Business: Communication strategist
Conclusion: The interview validated the need for a cash flow forecasting tool whose primary value is providing peace of mind and better tax management for freelancers.

Key Findings:

• Primary Value: Alleviating anxiety over quarterly tax prepayments, allowing for more precise financial management.

• Essential Features: Requires a 3-12 month forecast horizon, 'what if' scenario testing, historical trend analysis, and proactive tax-date alerts.

• Delivery: Must be a mobile-first experience with simple data visualizations (e.g., bar charts).

• Caution: Users with low financial literacy might mistake the forecast for reality, requiring clear product design. Sabine would not pay for the tool if its primary function was to upsell her on financial products.
Follow ups: Yes
Interview Date: 20 février 2026
Pain points: 
• Tax Anxiety & Over-Saving: She keeps excessive funds in her business account due to nervousness about quarterly tax prepayments, preventing more precise financial actions.
• Time-Consuming Manual Admin: Significant time is spent on invoicing, coordinating freelancer pay, and manually monitoring multiple bank accounts.
• Poor Financial Overview: Her reliance on a simple spreadsheet limits her overall clarity, especially regarding yearly income and future cash flow.
• Stress from Tax-Bucket Deficits: She experiences worry when she pays freelancers before clients pay her, causing temporary deficits in her tax savings account.
• Mobile-Only Frustration: She prefers mobile banking and finds the web-based app features inadequate, requiring simple data visualization on her phone.
