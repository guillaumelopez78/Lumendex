# Jay Holm

Business: Automotive sound equipment
Conclusion: Jay represents a sophisticated, growth-oriented user who manages a complex, seasonal business. Unlike users who prioritize manual control, Jay is eager for predictive automation that can help him navigate the risks of international credit and inventory planning.

For Jay, the value of an accounting tool is measured by its ability to centralize data and forecast the future. He is a prime candidate for advanced features like product-level analytics and automated liquidity alerts, provided they help him manage his 3–6 month seasonal horizon.

This interview features an interviewer from an accounting software company (likely Billi) and a business owner, Jay, who runs an export-focused company established in 2008 that sells automotive sound equipment for cars and motorcycles.

Business Profile & Context
• Operations: The company consists of two people in Denmark with external agents in the USA and Europe.

- Market: Almost all sales are exports to Europe and the US due to strict regulations and low demand in Denmark.
- Seasonality: The business is highly seasonal, peaking between March and October.
- Growth: They expect to exceed an 8 million DKK balance soon, which may trigger a requirement for external financial reviews by 2027-2028.

Liquidity Management & Challenges
• Current Practice: They manage liquidity primarily through their bank account and the "cheap" (Billi) platform to track invoices and customer debt.

- Credit Risks: They ship large containers to US customers on credit, receiving sporadic payments. European customers typically pay within 7 to 30 days.
- Order Planning: A significant amount of time is spent on planning orders and managing warehouse stock to ensure they have enough goods before the peak season begins.

Feedback on Liquidity Prognosis Feature
• Interest level: Jay finds the proposed liquidity tool interesting, particularly for its potential to use pattern recognition and algorithms to predict future cash flow based on historical data.

- Desired Timeframe: He prefers a forecast of 3 to 6 months (one to two quarters) to align with his seasonal production cycles and long lead times for imported products.
- Key Features Desired:
◦ Product-Level Insights: He strongly desires the ability to see which specific products are selling best within the accounting overview.

◦ Simulations: He is interested in "what-if" scenarios, such as simulating the impact of losing a percentage of expected income.

◦ Alerts: He would appreciate notifications for reaching specific liquidity limits or expected "overtakes".

◦ Actionable Proposals: He is open to the tool suggesting business actions, such as where to borrow money if liquidity drops below a certain level.

General Tool Feedback
• Sorting Preference: Jay requested a "flywheel" or default setting for sorting uploaded documents (e.g., always sorting by date or newest to oldest) so he doesn't have to manually click it every time.

- Integration: He expressed that while he uses a separate layer/warehouse management system, it would be much easier if inventory and invoicing were integrated into one system.---
