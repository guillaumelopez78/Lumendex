## ~~⚖️ The case for building our own platform~~

- **~~1. Ops costs optimization~~**
    
    We currently bear the full load of being a regulated institution in FR, managing all things that come with it (KYC, AML & other compliance obligations, banking ops, internal tooling…).
    
    We would be able to support Banking operations on all 4 markets without scaling our operations linearly, instead of having a large operation load in France + paying a premium to BAAS to offload the work to them.
    
- **~~2. Banking cost optimization~~**
    
    All payment operations have associated costs, on which BAAS add their margins. Of those, the most significant ones are:
    
    - Card creation & logistics
    - Card payment fees
    - KYC fees (for NL/DE)
    - Account fees
    - SEPA fees
    
    Combined, these costs represent most of the direct costs for Banking, and would be reduced by owning the platform (how much would depend on future negotiations with partners).
    
- **~~3. Banking revenue optimization~~**
    
    With our current setup, we share revenue with BAAS on 2 fronts:
    
    - Interchange (90% in FR; less in NL/DE)
    - Deposit interests (STR + 9bps in FR; less in NL/DE)
    
    Working directly with card schemes would unlock the missing 10% interchange, while migrating all deposit under the Shine licence would unlock the missing deposit revenue.
    
    These upsides would only grow with the increase in volumes as our customer base & bank volumes get larger. 
    
- **~~4. Bandwidth & speed~~**
    
    As long as we keep X platforms, we need to maintain the complexity that comes with having multiple implementations, the tech, ops, and partnership load that comes with it.
    
    Converging to a single platform would allow us to get the teams more focused, and free bandwidth to improve the product for customers, and fuel growth.
    
- **~~5. Freedom from BAAS limitations~~**
    
    When we rely on a BAAS, we’re constrained by their feature set, roadmap, way of doing things, and in the case of SWAN/Solaris, the fact that they are the ones responsible for the customer relationship, and compliance aspects.
    
    This means that there will always be a limit to what we can build, and the level of service we can provide to customers.
