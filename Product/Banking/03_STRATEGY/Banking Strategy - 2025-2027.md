# Banking strategy - 2025-2027

Last update: April 2025

## 🔮 The Vision for the Banking

To achieve our company mission, we want to offer a top tier banking experience to our European customers, on par with what customers can get from the best B2C banking products, providing everything small businesses need to manage their daily financial operations while simplifying their lives and reducing the time they spend on admin.

We aim at exploring how the integration of banking, invoicing and accounting could facilitate, and even automate, key financial workflows, like getting paid, paying salaries & suppliers, and getting accounting and taxes done.

## 🎯 Our long term objectives

Our strategy for banking is structured around 3 key axes, that will put us in a strong position as a key EU SME Banking player, while improving our revenue & costs structure:

- **Offering a common, and localized, Banking product on all of our 4 markets**
- **Have a single and efficient core banking system for our 4 markets**
    - Owned banking tech (no BAAS)
    - Optimized operations
    - A single, localized product
    - Improved revenue & cost structure
    - Improved customer experience
- **Create strong bonds between banking, and other core Ageras products**
    - Create the best accounting-banking integration on the market
    - Integrate our various ways to get paid into invoicing
    - Create bridges between our different offers, in a way that adapts to the customers journey; not forcing them into an ecosystem.

While delivering a highly available, easy to use, and secured service that our customers can trust and love, and improving our operational efficiency. 

Let’s first dive into why we think it’s a good idea to build our own platform

## ⚖️ The case for building our own platform

- **1. Ops costs optimization**
    
    We currently bear the full load of being a regulated institution in FR, managing all things that come with it (KYC, AML & other compliance obligations, banking ops, internal tooling…).
    
    We would be able to support Banking operations on all 4 markets without scaling our operations linearly, instead of having a large operation load in France + paying a premium to BAAS to offload the work to them.
    
- **2. Banking cost optimization**
    
    All payment operations have associated costs, on which BAAS add their margins. Of those, the most significant ones are:
    
    - Card creation & logistics
    - Card payment fees
    - KYC fees (for NL/DE)
    - Account fees
    - SEPA fees
    
    Combined, these costs represent most of the direct costs for Banking, and would be reduced by owning the platform (how much would depend on future negotiations with partners).
    
- **3. Banking revenue optimization**
    
    With our current setup, we share revenue with BAAS on 2 fronts:
    
    - Interchange (90% in FR; less in NL/DE)
    - Deposit interests (STR + 9bps in FR; less in NL/DE)
    
    Working directly with card schemes would unlock the missing 10% interchange, while migrating all deposit under the Shine licence would unlock the missing deposit revenue.
    
    These upsides would only grow with the increase in volumes as our customer base & bank volumes get larger. 
    
- **4. Bandwidth & speed**
    
    As long as we keep X platforms, we need to maintain the complexity that comes with having multiple implementations, the tech, ops, and partnership load that comes with it.
    
    Converging to a single platform would allow us to get the teams more focused, and free bandwidth to improve the product for customers, and fuel growth.
    
- **5. Freedom from BAAS limitations**
    
    When we rely on a BAAS, we’re constrained by their feature set, roadmap, way of doing things, and in the case of SWAN/Solaris, the fact that they are the ones responsible for the customer relationship, and compliance aspects.
    
    This means that there will always be a limit to what we can build, and the level of service we can provide to customers.
    

There are risks of course:

- It’s a significant investment, both in terms of people time building this, and in terms of consulting fees/legal fees/upfront partner fees
- It’s not trivial stuff, and getting things wrong comes with financial & reputation risk.

## 🗺️ How we plan to achieve this

There will be multiple steps before we reach the long term objectives.

1. We decide to **focus first on building of a unified banking offer that scales on 4 markets** before integrating banking service in a single product alongside accounting and invoicing features.
2. We acknowledge that our current banking stacks (one different setup per country) limits our capacity to iterate fast and operate efficiently. So we want to **unify our systems on a single core banking system that will let us operate at scale in a multi-currency environment**.
3. We want to expand our capability to **operate as a Payment Institution in our 4 markets** (as we do today in France) to get more control on the overall banking experience (onboarding, compliance monitoring) while increasing our revenue (reduced margin from our partners, deposit revenue, interchange revenue).
4. We decide to **focus on German and French markets first**, before Netherlands and Denmark.
5. And we want to build a **one banking user experience under the same brand**, that will later fit with the one product experience

This is a multi-year journey, with 3 parallel streams that all start now, while will be release at different times.

## What to expect in **2025**

In 2025, we will focus on

- fully casting off Société Générale compliance tools (target Q2 2025)
- fulfilling our compliance and regulatory obligations in France (target Q2 2025)
- supporting growth in FR and DE with a new pricing and a few highly-expected features (target Q2 2025)
- aligning our UI in FR and DE on a similar UX and brand (will span over to 2026)
- better understanding of our customers needs across all markets (evergreen)
- preparing our process and tools to manage KYC, Compliance and Banking operations in DE (will span over to 2026)
- building the unified core banking system (will span over to 2026)
- improving the efficiency of our ops teams (evergreen)
- improving the quality and reliability of our systems to increase the availability of our services (evergreen)

That’s a lot to unpack, but we firmly believe that we have the talent and knowledge to answer those questions and pull this off, and that 2025 is going to be an exciting, and pivotal year for Ageras.
